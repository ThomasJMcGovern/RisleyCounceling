QUESTIONS

01. WHERE IS YOUR THERAPY PRACTICE LOCATED?
Our practice is located in Los Angeles, California. We offer both in-person sessions at our office and teletherapy options for California residents.
02. WHERE DO YOU PARK?
There is ample street parking available around our office. We also have a dedicated parking lot for clients with clearly marked spaces.
03. WHO DO YOU SEE IN YOUR PRIVATE PRACTICE?
We work with individuals, couples, and families. Our practice specializes in relationship issues, anxiety, depression, trauma, life transitions, and cultural identity concerns. We welcome clients from all backgrounds and identities.
04. HOW LONG IS A SESSION AND HOW OFTEN WILL I MEET WITH YOU?
Individual sessions are 50 minutes, while couples and family sessions may be 75-90 minutes. We typically meet weekly, especially at the beginning of therapy, but can adjust the frequency based on your needs and progress.
05. HOW LONG DOES THERAPY TAKE?
The length of therapy varies depending on your goals, the complexity of the issues you're working on, and your commitment to the process. Some clients achieve their goals in a few months, while others benefit from longer-term support.
06. DO YOU ACCEPT INSURANCE?
We are out-of-network providers. We can provide you with a superbill that you can submit to your insurance for potential reimbursement. We recommend checking with your insurance provider about out-of-network benefits.
07. DO YOU MEET WITH INDIVIDUALS AS A MARRIAGE AND FAMILY THERAPIST?
Yes, Marriage and Family Therapists are trained to work with individuals, couples, and families. We take a systemic approach, considering how relationships and environment impact individual well-being.
08. HOW DO I GET STARTED?
Contact us through our website or phone to schedule a free 15-minute consultation. This allows us to learn about your needs and ensure we're a good fit before scheduling your first session.
09. WHAT WILL MY FIRST SESSION BE LIKE?
Your first session is an opportunity to share your story and what brings you to therapy. We'll discuss your goals, gather relevant history, and start developing a therapeutic plan together. It's also a time to ask any questions you have about the process.
10. IF WE KNOW EACH OTHER PERSONALLY, WILL YOU SEE ME AS A CLIENT?
To maintain professional boundaries and ensure the best therapeutic experience, we do not see clients with whom we have personal relationships. We're happy to provide referrals to other qualified therapists in these situations.
11. DO I REALLY NEED THERAPY? I CAN USUALLY HANDLE MY PROBLEMS ON MY OWN.
While self-reliance is valuable, therapy can provide additional tools and perspectives for personal growth. It's not about inability to handle problems, but rather about having professional support to work through challenges more effectively.
12. HOW MUCH DOES THERAPY COST PER SESSION?
Our current session fees are [Insert fee range]. The exact cost depends on the type and length of session. We're happy to discuss fees during your initial consultation.
13. DO YOU OFFER A SLIDING SCALE FOR YOUR FEES?
We offer a limited number of sliding scale spots based on financial need and availability. Please inquire about current sliding scale openings during your consultation.
14. WHAT FORMS OF PAYMENT DO YOU ACCEPT?
We accept credit cards, HSA/FSA cards, and electronic payments. Payment is due at the time of service.
15. WHAT IS THE "NO SURPRISES ACT NOTICE" AND GOOD FAITH ESTIMATE?
The No Surprises Act requires healthcare providers to provide a Good Faith Estimate of expected charges for services. We will provide this estimate before or during your first appointment and update it annually or when services change significantly.
